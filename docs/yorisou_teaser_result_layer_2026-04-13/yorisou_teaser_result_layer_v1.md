## Teaser Layer Operating Rules

- The teaser must recognize the user immediately.
- The teaser must surface one tension or contradiction, not a full explanation.
- The teaser may be lightly witty, but not joke-first.
- The teaser must be native Japanese, modern, and mobile-readable.
- The teaser must be screenshot-capable in one glance.
- The teaser must not sound like a survey, a counselor, or an AI assistant.
- The teaser must preserve persona separation.
- The teaser must leave room for share-card phrasing and deep-report expansion.

## Verification of Persona / Dimension Anchors

The teaser layer uses the same canonical dimension meanings as the result structure spec.

- `D13 / D20 / D21`: change tolerance, decision latency, and action threshold stay distinct.
- `D03 / D15`: task switching and concurrent load stay distinct.
- `D04 / D06`: shopping priority and order/placement stay distinct.
- `D07 / D08 / D19`: home hazard checking, threshold mobility, and reassurance gathering stay distinct.

## Teaser Calibration Scale

Use a 1–5 scale.

- `5`: strong, native, precise, screenshot-ready
- `4`: good, only minor polishing needed
- `3`: usable but slightly flat or slightly too broad
- `2`: weak, drifting, or too generic
- `1`: unusable

Recommended lines below have been revised to sit at `4` or `5`.

## Teaser-Layer Boundary Notes

- The teaser must feel complete without mythology names.
- The teaser may imply character, but not lore.
- The teaser must be short enough to screenshot, but distinct enough to separate neighboring personas.
- The teaser must hand off naturally into share-card wording without becoming share-card wording.
- The teaser must leave one step of semantic room for the deep report.

## Global Anti-Patterns

- Generic psychology phrasing
- Counselor-like phrasing
- Corporate-soft phrasing
- Joke-first phrasing that weakens the persona
- Meme slang that dates quickly
- Abstract “you are this type” language with no scene
- Copy that could fit two neighboring personas equally well
