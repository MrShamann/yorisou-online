# YORISOU — Users and Problem

*(Package 20, 2026-07-15 · canonical)*

## Primary users

Japanese-speaking adults on their phones who want to understand themselves and feel accompanied — without the weight of clinical care, the exposure of social media, or the emptiness of throwaway quizzes. They arrive curious ("what am I like?"), and some stay for companionship. Secondary: the founder/operators running consented pilots and paid-report operations under documented quality and consent gates.

## The problem

There is a gap between "I'm fine" and "I need professional help" where most people live most of the time. In Japan that gap is underserved: counseling carries stigma and cost; personality quizzes are entertainment that forgets you; social apps demand performance. People want **gentle, private, continuing understanding** — in Japanese, on LINE, at their own pace.

## What users need (and YORISOU's answer)

| Need | YORISOU answer |
|---|---|
| "Understand me without judging me" | a personality test written with warmth, mobile-first, in natural Japanese |
| "Tell me honestly, not mystically" | results as *interpretation* with visible confidence and limits — never fake scientific precision |
| "Don't trap or pressure me" | optional progression, zero-pressure next actions, everything declinable |
| "Stay with me" | LINE as the continuing-companion channel — the relationship, not a broadcast list |
| "Protect what I told you" | explicit consent gates, privacy-first data governance, documented non-use policies for sensitive report data |

## Hard boundaries

- **No medical/diagnostic/treatment framing** — including in marketing, results, and LINE copy.
- **Emotional safety over engagement** — if a design choice trades user calm for metrics, it loses.
- **No automatic LINE sending** without explicit product authorization; consent precedes contact.
- Public product language is **Japanese**; internal/engineering language may be English.
