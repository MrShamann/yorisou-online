# YORISOU — Product Vision

*(Package 20, 2026-07-15 · canonical)*

## What YORISOU is

YORISOU（寄り添う — to stay close beside someone）is a **Japanese-language, mobile-first emotional-companionship product**. It begins with a personality test that makes people feel *seen* — and continues as a gentle, ongoing relationship through **LINE**, the channel where Japanese users actually live.

The test is the **acquisition and understanding entrypoint, not the entire product**. What YORISOU sells is not a score; it is the feeling of being understood over time: thoughtful result storytelling, gentle check-ins, and paid detailed reports produced under explicit consent and human review.

## What YORISOU is not

- **Not medical.** No diagnosis, no treatment framing, no clinical language — ever. YORISOU explains its interpretations as *interpretation*, never as objective truth about a person's mind.
- **Not a gamified quiz farm.** No streaks, no addiction loops, no dark conversion patterns.
- **Not a generic chatbot.** The relationship is structured (test → result story → LINE companionship → consented deeper reports), not an open-ended AI chat.

## The relationship arc

1. **Meet** — a person takes the test on their phone (Japanese, low-pressure, skippable depth).
2. **Feel understood** — a staged, story-like result reveal (summary first, honest confidence, visible limits, gentle next actions).
3. **Stay close** — they add YORISOU on LINE; continuing companionship happens there at their pace, with explicit consent for anything deeper.
4. **Go deeper (optional, paid)** — detailed reports under strict consent, quality rubrics, and human review (the `docs/` operations corpus governs this).

## Why this matters

Japan has deep demand for low-barrier emotional support that is neither clinical care nor social-media exposure. YORISOU's bet: a warm, honest, consent-first companion in the user's own messaging app can hold that middle ground — commercially through paid depth, ethically through boundaries that never medicalize or manipulate.
