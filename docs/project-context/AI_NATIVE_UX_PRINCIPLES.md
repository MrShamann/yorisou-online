# YORISOU — AI-Native UX Principles (permanent)

*(Package 20, 2026-07-15 · canonical)*

1. **AI speaks as interpretation, never as truth.** Every AI-derived statement about a person is framed as a reading（解釈）— with honest confidence bands in words, never fake numeric precision. The Package 9 result grammar is the model: 回答から (from your answers) / タイプ解釈 (type interpretation) / 限界 (limits) — and when AI was not involved, that is truthfully shown too.
2. **Never medical.** No diagnostic vocabulary, severity scores, condition names, or treatment suggestions — in results, LINE copy, marketing, or code-level strings.
3. **Consent precedes depth.** Deeper analysis, data retention, report generation, and any LINE contact each require explicit, specific, revocable consent. Consent copy is a governed product surface (see the `docs/` consent corpus), not boilerplate.
4. **Optional progression.** Every step is skippable; every action declinable; the product must remain kind to the user who does nothing.
5. **Emotional safety outranks engagement.** No streaks, no scarcity timers, no guilt copy, no re-engagement pressure. If a metric needs manipulation to move, the metric loses.
6. **Progressive disclosure.** Summary first, depth on request — server-rendered content first, enhancement layered on top (the result reveal is a progressive enhancement over fully server-rendered content).
7. **Privacy by architecture.** Sensitive report data has documented storage/retention/non-use boundaries; nothing about a person is shared, compared, or benchmarked publicly.
8. **Human review on paid depth.** Paid detailed reports pass quality rubrics and human review before delivery — AI drafts, humans stand behind it.
9. **No automatic external sending.** LINE messages are never sent automatically without explicit product authorization; the runtime boundary stays untouched until a founder package changes it.
10. **No autonomous writes.** No AI agent may claim standing permission to change product state; the question set, scoring, and taxonomy are contract-tested and change only through founder-authorized packages.
