## 1. Purpose

This doctrine defines why Yorisou exists, what strategic problem it addresses, how it differs from conventional social and recommendation platforms, how its platform compounds, and how it should sequence market expansion.

It prevents Yorisou from drifting into a test site, generic community, content feed, affiliate catalog, or overbuilt Japan-market product.

---

## 2. Strategic Position

Yorisou's strategic target is a Japan-market, Japanese-language, AI-native, state-based, low-pressure social discovery and recommendation platform.

The current v0.3 work is a governed transition toward that target. It must not be represented as already operating at global social-platform scale.

It helps users understand their current state, express lived experience safely, discover relevant experiences and resources, take useful next steps, and return when their state changes.

Japan is the first operating and validation market.

Yorisou is architected for future portability while currently governed and operated for Japan.

---

## 3. Strategic Problem

Modern digital products usually begin from one of five assumptions:

- commerce begins with inventory,
- media begins with content,
- social begins with sharing,
- search begins with keywords,
- professional support begins with a defined problem.

Many users do not begin with any of these.

They begin with an unclear state.

They may not know what to search for, what category they belong to, or what action would help.

Conventional social media adds three further problems:

1. users feel pressure to perform a stable public identity,
2. algorithms reward attention, comparison, and popularity,
3. useful lived experience is difficult to find by current state and situation.

Yorisou starts from state, not status.

---

## 4. Core Strategic Insight

A person's current state is not the same as a permanent personality.

A useful recommendation depends not only on who someone is, but also on:

- what is happening now,
- what they have already tried,
- what degree of interaction they can tolerate,
- what context they are in,
- what kind of next step is realistic.

The strategic opportunity is to connect:

> current state + lived experience + trusted next step

Yorisou can create a more useful system than a generic content feed by organizing experience around state and outcome.

---

## 5. Strategic Thesis

Yorisou's v0.3 thesis is:

> People often know their current state before they know what they need. Yorisou helps them understand that state, safely express or discover relevant lived experience, and identify a useful next step without the pressure and noise of conventional social media.

The platform becomes more valuable as it learns:

- which experiences are relevant to which states,
- what users save,
- what users actually try,
- what users later report as helpful,
- how state changes alter relevance,
- where resources or services remain missing.

---

## 6. Strategic Differentiation

### 6.1 From Personality-Test Products

Tests are entry and understanding tools, not the entire product.

Yorisou does not permanently label users.

It uses existing deep and lightweight test assets to support state understanding, discovery, and recommendation.

### 6.2 From Conventional Social Media

Yorisou does not lead with followers, public identity, popularity, unrestricted posting, or infinite feeds.

It leads with structured experience, controlled visibility, limited discovery, and useful feedback.

### 6.3 From Search Engines

Search engines respond to explicit queries.

Yorisou can help when the user has a state but no clear query.

### 6.4 From Generic AI Companions

Yorisou does not depend on open-ended conversation alone.

It uses structured state, experience, recommendation, feedback, and evidence models.

### 6.5 From Recommendation Platforms

Yorisou ranks user fit first, quality second, and commercial value third.

It connects recommendation to state, experience, and feedback rather than only interest or purchase history.

---

## 7. Strategic Flywheel

The v0.3 flywheel is:

1. user enters through a check, experience, search, share, channel, or recommendation,
2. Yorisou builds or updates current-state context,
3. user privately records or safely shares experience,
4. other users discover relevant experience,
5. users save, try, respond, or reject,
6. Yorisou recommends a limited next step,
7. users reflect on outcome,
8. signals improve state, experience, and recommendation models,
9. higher-quality discovery increases return and trust,
10. aggregated signals produce product, partner, and public-value intelligence.

Short form:

> State -> Experience -> Discovery -> Action -> Outcome -> Intelligence -> Better Fit

---

## 8. First Strategic Proofs

Yorisou must prove, in order:

### Proof 1: State Entry

Users understand the entry proposition and complete a lightweight state interaction.

### Proof 2: Private Return Value

Users save or revisit private state information.

### Proof 3: Experience Value

Users find structured experiences relevant enough to save or try.

### Proof 4: Outcome Feedback

Users report whether something was useful, not merely whether they liked it.

### Proof 5: Contribution

Some users create safe structured experience cards.

### Proof 6: Recommendation Fit

State-based recommendations outperform generic browsing.

### Proof 7: Commercial Value

Users pay for deeper reports, premium reflection, or relevant services without manipulation.

### Proof 8: Intelligence Value

Aggregated signals improve founder decisions or support credible external insight.

---

## 9. Critical Assumptions

### Assumption 1

Users prefer a low-pressure experience enough to return even without conventional social rewards.

### Assumption 2

Structured experience cards are easier to create and more useful to consume than generic posts.

### Assumption 3

Users will provide outcome feedback such as saved, tried, helpful, or not relevant.

### Assumption 4

Life-state context improves discovery and recommendation quality.

### Assumption 5

Users trust private-by-default and scoped visibility controls.

### Assumption 6

The existing test universe can become a recurring entry system rather than a one-time funnel.

### Assumption 7

AI can organize and match experiences without fabricating, overgeneralizing, or exposing users.

### Assumption 8

Japan can validate the model before wider language-market expansion.

## 10. Market Strategy

### 10.1 Japan Market Focus

Yorisou v0.3 focuses on Japan.

The product should be designed around Japanese user behavior, Japanese-natural language, LINE-based return paths, and the trust expectations of the Japanese consumer market.

### 10.2 Channel Strategy

The core acquisition and return channels are:

- Web;
- LINE;
- shareable result and experience links;
- email registration and account recovery;
- later social distribution where useful.

Yorisou should not create separate country sites, foreign-language communities, or international operating programs during the current stage.

### 10.3 Future Optionality

Technical choices should avoid making later localization impossible, but future optionality must not create present-day complexity. Japan product proof comes first.

## 11. Brand Strategy

YORISOU remains the active product brand.

The active product domain remains yorisou.online.

The v0.3 phase does not include renaming, rebranding, creating a separate international master brand, or moving to another country-specific domain.

Brand work should focus on making YORISOU understandable, memorable, trustworthy, and visually distinctive in Japan.

## 12. Business Strategy

First revenue priority remains asset-light and user-aligned:

1. digital reports,
2. premium reflection and state history,
3. curated recommendation and referral,
4. selected partner services,
5. validated products,
6. demand intelligence.

Yorisou should not rely on:

- advertising-feed scale,
- creator-rank economics,
- inventory-first commerce,
- aggressive subscriptions before return value,
- hidden sponsorship.

---

## 13. Content and Community Strategy

The first content supply should be controlled and structured.

Initial content may come from:

- founder/editorial demo content,
- approved synthetic examples clearly labeled,
- invited contributors,
- reviewed user submissions,
- existing Yorisou result and report material.

Yorisou should not open unrestricted public posting before:

- visibility controls,
- reporting,
- deletion,
- moderation,
- content-safety review,
- support handling,
- evidence tracking

are operational.

---

## 14. Defensibility

Yorisou's defensibility should come from the combined system, not from one feature.

Defensible assets include:

- life-state model and longitudinal state data,
- structured experience schema,
- state-to-experience relevance data,
- save/try/helpfulness outcome graph,
- recommendation reasoning and feedback,
- trust and visibility infrastructure,
- culturally adapted content systems,
- governed AI workflows,
- anonymized demand intelligence.

A feed, test, or chatbot alone is not defensible.

---

## 15. Strategic Red Lines

Yorisou must not:

- optimize primarily for time spent,
- use vulnerability to drive conversion,
- reward public oversharing,
- create permanent identity traps,
- hide commercial relationships,
- treat weak engagement as validated demand,
- launch multiple markets before operational readiness,
- build a full social network before proving structured experience value,
- claim that AI-generated or synthetic experience is real user experience.

---

## 16. Capability-Stage Discipline

All strategic communication should use one of these labels:

- **Current:** already implemented and operational.
- **MVP:** approved for the next bounded validation build.
- **Planned:** intended after MVP evidence.
- **Validated:** supported by meaningful behavioral or commercial evidence.
- **Long-term:** possible future branch.
- **Deferred / Rejected:** not part of the active path.

Japan operation is Current/MVP depending on the specific surface. Foreign-language and overseas community operation are outside the v0.3 scope.

---

## 17. Strategic Sequence

The preferred sequence is:

1. approve v0.3 governance,
2. audit existing assets and code against v0.3,
3. preserve valid test and report assets,
4. implement private state space,
5. implement structured experience cards,
6. implement limited state-based discovery,
7. implement save/try/helpfulness feedback,
8. strengthen recommendation and return loops,
9. validate Japan market behavior,
10. prepare measured language-market expansion.

## 18. AI-Native Strategic Advantage

YORISOU's defensibility does not come from reproducing a conventional social feed. It comes from the governed interaction of:

- Life-State Graph;
- Experience Graph;
- Personal Memory Graph;
- Recommendation Graph;
- Community and Local Graph;
- Product Opportunity Graph;
- Companion Layer;
- Agent Operating Layer.

The strategic compounding loop is:

> State and experience signals -> AI organization -> verified discovery and recommendation -> user action and feedback -> better state, memory, community, local, and product intelligence -> improved future experience.

The platform must become more useful through accumulated permissioned context, not through surveillance, compulsive engagement, or indiscriminate data collection.

## 19. AI-Generated and Continuously Updated Product Surface

Approved Agents may continuously create or update:

- test-question candidates and short-check variants;
- experience summaries and state-topic pages;
- limited daily and weekly discovery sets;
- books, music, video, podcast, tool, public-resource, and local-place candidates;
- user-specific recommendation explanations;
- local and community topic candidates;
- internal demand intelligence;
- product concepts and pre-commerce validation drafts.

Core methodology, sensitive policies, major commercial promises, production code, and high-risk external actions remain governed and cannot be autonomously changed.

## 20. Companion, Community, Local, and Real-World Movement

AI companionship must help users move among private reflection, useful content, real people, local places, and practical action. It must not trap users in a closed AI relationship.

Community formation should be dynamic and state-relevant rather than follower-based. Local discovery should combine current state, user-selected geography, desired social intensity, time, cost, and verified availability.

## 21. Product Opportunity Strategy

YORISOU may use aggregated, anonymized, and evidence-labeled signals to identify unmet product and service needs. Agents may produce product concepts, design candidates, price hypotheses, landing-page drafts, waitlists, and pre-sale validation plans.

No Agent may independently collect money, contract suppliers, make binding claims, or launch a commercial product without founder authorization and the required operational controls.
