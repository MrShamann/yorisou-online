## 1. Purpose

This policy governs how YORISOU turns aggregated Japanese-market signals into internal insight and possible external reports without exposing individual users.

## 2. Permitted Signals

Permitted aggregate signals may include:

- topic frequency;
- save, try, helpful, not-for-me, hide, and report rates;
- broad state-context patterns;
- unmet resource needs;
- content gaps;
- report or test demand;
- regional patterns only where sample size and privacy permit.

## 3. Prohibited Externalization

External reports must not contain:

- identifiable user records;
- raw private notes;
- private test answers;
- small-cell combinations that enable re-identification;
- exact quotations without appropriate visibility and reuse permission;
- unverified causal claims;
- claims that YORISOU represents all Japanese users.

## 4. Evidence Labels

Every material finding must be labeled by source and confidence, such as:

- observed platform behavior;
- self-reported user feedback;
- user-generated experience;
- public external source;
- internal hypothesis;
- limited sample.

## 5. Minimum Quality Gate

Before external release, a report must pass:

- privacy review;
- evidence review;
- sample-size and re-identification check;
- commercial-interest disclosure;
- capability and limitation review;
- founder approval.

## 6. Japan Scope

v0.3 reports concern the current Japanese-language product and its actual user base. They must not be described as global insight or cross-cultural evidence.

## 7. User Benefit

Demand intelligence should improve product relevance, expose unmet needs, guide useful resource development, or support public value. It must not exist solely to monetize sensitive life states.
