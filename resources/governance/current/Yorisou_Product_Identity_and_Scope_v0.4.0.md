# YORISOU Product Identity and Scope v0.4.0

**Status:** Approved
**Pack:** YORISOU Governance Pack v0.4.0 · **Drafted:** 2026-07-13 · **Approved as activation candidate:** 2026-07-14 · **Approver:** Edward

## 1. Purpose
Fix the product identity hierarchy so surfaces cannot drift or conflict.

## 2. Scope
All user-facing surfaces, current and planned.

## 3. Binding rules
1. Core product: Calm Emotional Companionship — a quiet companion that remembers only what the user chose to keep and permits, and helps them meet it again.
2. Relationship tests, persona results, and reports are **subordinate acquisition and commercial surfaces**: they may attract, delight, and monetize, but never define the brand promise above companionship and never inject data into companion memory outside the explicit consent path.
3. Social discovery and UGC are **deferred and constrained**: existing surfaces run behind the `legacy_social` flag, receive no new investment, are excluded from companion surfaces, and are re-evaluated only under Physical–Digital Expression governance.
4. The first-use promise is the validated one: leave a little → return in 7–14 days → quietly compare then and now. Copy expressing this follows Experience Architecture Doctrine.
5. Scope exclusions: YORISOU is not therapy, diagnosis, crisis support, a general chatbot, or a social network.

## 4. Prohibited behavior
Marketing the test line as the core product; linking test/social surfaces into memory flows except the single governed entry point; therapy or clinical positioning; engagement-pressure mechanics anywhere.

## 5. Decision authority
Identity hierarchy changes: Edward only.

## 6. Required evidence
Surface inventory review at every Release Gate confirming subordination and flag states.

## 7. Exceptions
None.

## 8. Change control
Change Management v1.0.
