## 1. Role of Reports

Digital reports remain a valid YORISOU product, but they are no longer the sole center of the platform. Reports deepen the user's private understanding and connect state signals to reflection and optional next steps.

## 2. Report Principles

A report must be:

- private by default;
- based on disclosed inputs;
- non-clinical and non-deterministic;
- clear about uncertainty;
- useful without requiring another purchase;
- compatible with the user's ability to correct or delete underlying data.

## 3. Report Structure

A report may include:

- current-state overview;
- patterns across dimensions;
- tensions and supporting conditions;
- reflection questions;
- small next steps;
- selected experience or resource recommendations;
- change-over-time comparison where valid.

## 4. Report and Social Separation

Private report content must not automatically become an experience card, public result, social profile, or recommendation signal visible to others.

Users may choose to create a separate public-safe expression, but the conversion must be explicit and previewable.

## 5. Commercial Honesty

Paid reports must clearly state product scope, generation method, limitations, delivery format, and refund/cancellation conditions. Reports must not be sold as diagnosis, certification, or guaranteed decision support.
