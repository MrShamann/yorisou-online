# YORISOU Community, Local, and Real-World Connection Policy v0.1

**Status:** Approved  
**Market:** Japan

## 1. Purpose

YORISOU connects private reflection and AI companionship to useful human experience, community, local places, activities, and public resources. AI companionship must not be designed to isolate users from real relationships.

## 2. Community Model

Community is organized around temporary states, situations, experiences, and practical needs rather than permanent identity labels or follower competition.

YORISOU may provide:

- state topics;
- structured experience collections;
- low-pressure reactions;
- finite discussion prompts;
- temporary or revisable topic participation;
- locally relevant discovery.

Unrestricted private messaging is outside the initial scope.

## 3. AI Community Support

Agents may:

- detect repeated themes;
- merge duplicate topics;
- summarize experience with provenance labels;
- recommend relevant topics;
- identify harmful or spam patterns;
- suggest topic lifecycle changes.

Agents must not fabricate community activity, user testimony, or consensus.

## 4. Local Discovery

Local recommendations may include:

- quiet places;
- libraries and public facilities;
- exhibitions and small events;
- low-interaction activities;
- community resources;
- locally available products or services.

Matching may consider user-selected area, desired social intensity, time, budget, accessibility, and current state.

Precise user location must not be exposed to other users. Geographic granularity must be user controlled.

## 5. Real-World Safety

YORISOU must not imply that a place, event, provider, or user is fully verified unless the stated verification was actually performed. External availability, price, accessibility, and safety information must be timestamped and sourced where practical.

## 6. Partner and Commercial Boundary

Organic, public, partner, affiliate, and YORISOU-owned resources must be distinguishable. Agents may discover candidates but may not contact, contract, or commercially commit without authorization.
