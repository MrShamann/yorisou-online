## 1. Purpose

This policy governs AI used in testing, state summaries, experience-card assistance, discovery, recommendation, moderation support, reports, and internal operations.

AI supports user understanding and platform operations. It does not replace user judgment, professional authority, or human accountability.

## 2. Permitted AI Uses

AI may:

- organize a user's own words into a clearer experience card;
- summarize test or state signals using approved methodology;
- suggest non-deterministic reflection prompts;
- rank a finite set of relevant experiences and resources;
- detect spam, obvious harassment, personal-information leakage, and prohibited content for review or automatic restriction;
- translate or normalize internal metadata where meaning is preserved;
- assist internal analysis and draft generation.

## 3. Prohibited AI Uses

AI must not:

- invent a user experience, result, testimonial, partner, or evidence;
- present medical, psychological, legal, financial, immigration, employment, or welfare judgment as authoritative;
- expose another user's inferred state or private data;
- manipulate vulnerable users into purchases or continued engagement;
- create fake social proof;
- rewrite user content in a way that changes material facts;
- generate public content under a user's identity without explicit confirmation;
- use private content to create external reports unless the approved aggregation and consent conditions are satisfied.

## 4. User-Controlled AI Assistance

When AI helps draft or rewrite an experience card:

1. the original user remains the author;
2. the user must preview the final text;
3. no publication occurs without the user's explicit action;
4. the user may edit, reject, or delete the output;
5. material facts may not be added;
6. sensitive details should be removed or generalized.

## 5. Recommendation Rules

Recommendations must be finite, explainable, and appropriate to the user's current context.

Every recommendation surface should state, in natural Japanese:

- why the item appeared;
- what type of item it is;
- whether it is YORISOU content, an organic external resource, a public resource, or commercial content;
- that it is optional and not a professional instruction.

Recommendation quality is evaluated through save, try, helpful, not-for-me, hide, and report signals—not through compulsive engagement time.

## 6. Moderation Assistance

YORISOU does not require pre-publication human approval for ordinary user posts.

Automated controls may hold, restrict, or remove content when there is a strong signal of:

- spam or scams;
- harassment or threats;
- disclosure of personal information;
- impersonation;
- illegal content;
- sexual exploitation content;
- direct self-harm encouragement;
- malicious automation.

Users must have access to report, block, delete, and visibility controls. Borderline cases may enter a review queue after publication.

## 7. Model and Provider Governance

AI providers must be replaceable. Private user content must not be sent to a provider unless the processing purpose, retention condition, and data handling are approved.

Provider logs, prompts, model version, failure mode, and fallback behavior must be auditable for material user-facing outputs.

## 8. Capability Honesty

AI-generated or AI-assisted content must be labeled when omission would mislead users. Demo content must be labeled as demo content. AI must never present future capability as currently available.

## 9. Autonomous Generation and Updating

Agents may autonomously generate or refresh low-risk candidates such as:

- recommendation candidates;
- resource metadata;
- content summaries;
- topic-page drafts;
- local discovery candidates;
- short-check and test-question candidates;
- product opportunity notes;
- internal intelligence.

Automatic public publication requires a separately approved workflow. Core methodology, binding claims, commercial launches, user identity actions, and Digital Legacy behavior cannot be changed autonomously.

## 10. Companion AI Governance

Companion AI must:

- disclose that it is AI;
- respect user-selected mode and quiet periods;
- avoid dependency, exclusivity, guilt, jealousy, or abandonment tactics;
- encourage appropriate real-world support where relevant;
- avoid claiming human consciousness, emotion, or authority;
- use memory only within user permissions;
- distinguish recollection from inference.

## 11. Test Evolution Governance

AI may propose, rewrite, classify, de-duplicate, and evaluate test questions. It may not directly modify the 120-question core methodology or publish unvalidated scoring changes. Test candidates require versioning, sandbox evaluation, and approved rollout rules.

## 12. Synthetic Identity and Digital Legacy

AI-generated representations of a real person, living or deceased, require explicit authorization and persistent disclosure. The system must prohibit unauthorized voice or image cloning, impersonation, invented memories, and new binding intentions attributed to the represented person.
