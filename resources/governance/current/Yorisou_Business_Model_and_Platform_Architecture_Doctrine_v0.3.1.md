## 1. Purpose

This doctrine translates the v0.3 strategic identity into a practical business and platform architecture.

It defines user entry, state understanding, private state, structured experience, discovery, low-pressure connection, recommendation, reports, trust, intelligence, commercialization, localization, and MVP sequencing.

---

## 2. Business Model Summary

Yorisou is a Japan-market, Japanese-language, state-based low-pressure social discovery and recommendation platform operated through one shared core with responsive Web and LINE-oriented interfaces and planned iOS and Android applications.

It creates value through:

1. current-state understanding,
2. private reflection and state history,
3. structured lived-experience expression,
4. state-based discovery,
5. limited and low-pressure connection,
6. trusted next-step recommendation,
7. digital reports and premium reflection,
8. resource and service matching,
9. anonymized demand intelligence,
10. later partner, product, and public-value pathways.

The commercial core remains asset-light during the early phase.

---

## 3. Core Business Flywheel

The v0.3 flywheel is:

1. user enters,
2. state context is understood,
3. user records or shares experience,
4. relevant experience is discovered,
5. user saves or tries something,
6. outcome feedback is recorded,
7. recommendation quality improves,
8. return value increases,
9. reports and services become more relevant,
10. anonymized intelligence becomes more credible.

> State -> Experience -> Discovery -> Action -> Outcome -> Recommendation -> Return -> Intelligence

## 4. Platform Layers

### 4.1 Entry and Channel Layer

Entry surfaces may include:

- Web,
- LINE,
- shared result or experience links,
- search,
- QR,
- Google,
- Apple,
- email magic link,
- future WhatsApp, WeChat, or regional channels.

The first experience should not require forced account creation.

Channel adapters must remain separate from core logic.

### 4.2 Life-State Understanding Layer

Purpose:

- identify current state,
- preserve state change,
- improve discovery and recommendation.

Inputs may include:

- deep assessment,
- short checks,
- relationship flows,
- reflection entries,
- fit checks,
- state notes,
- behavioral feedback.

Outputs may include:

- state tags,
- state confidence,
- rhythm indicators,
- current context,
- private interpretation,
- recommended next path.

### 4.3 Private State Space

Purpose:

- give the user personal value independent of public participation.

May include:

- current state,
- recent checks,
- private notes,
- saved experiences,
- intended actions,
- tried actions,
- state history,
- private reports,
- next check-in.

It is not a public profile or dashboard.

### 4.4 Experience Expression Layer

The primary social object is the Experience Card.

Core fields:

- state context,
- situation,
- action tried,
- perceived outcome,
- possible relevance,
- limitations,
- visibility,
- freshness,
- user-controlled edit or delete status.

Experience cards may use PRIVATE, INVITE_ONLY, SIMILAR_STATE_ONLY, ANONYMOUS_SHARED, PSEUDONYMOUS_SHARED, or PUBLIC_SAFE visibility. Public anonymity is a user-facing identity treatment, not the absence of an internal safety and audit reference.

AI may assist organization and de-identification but may not invent facts.

### 4.5 State-Based Discovery Layer

Purpose:

- help users discover experience and resources relevant to current state.

Discovery should prioritize:

1. state relevance,
2. context relevance,
3. freshness,
4. quality and trust,
5. outcome evidence,
6. diversity and non-repetition,
7. commercial value only after qualification.

The initial discovery model should be limited, not infinite.

Example daily set:

- three relevant experiences,
- one useful resource,
- one possible next step.

### 4.6 Low-Pressure Connection Layer

Allowed early interactions:

- save,
- try intent,
- tried,
- helpful,
- not relevant now,
- constrained supportive response,
- follow a state topic,
- receive a future check-in,
- invite-only sharing.

Deferred interactions:

- open comments,
- direct messages,
- public followers,
- creator ranks,
- broad user search,
- unrestricted groups.

### 4.7 Recommendation and Action Layer

Recommendation objects may include:

- experiences,
- small actions,
- tests,
- reflection prompts,
- digital reports,
- articles,
- books,
- podcasts,
- creators,
- apps,
- tools,
- places,
- public resources,
- partner services,
- concept products,
- future local support paths.

Every recommendation should preserve:

- reason,
- source,
- object type,
- commercial status,
- disclosure requirement,
- action,
- feedback,
- timestamp.

### 4.8 Digital Report and Premium Reflection Layer

Reports remain an early revenue path.

v0.3 report value should include:

- deeper state interpretation,
- state change over time,
- reflection prompts,
- related experience patterns,
- limited recommendations,
- practical next steps.

The free experience must remain useful.

### 4.9 Trust, Moderation, and Visibility Layer

Required capabilities:

- visibility selection,
- public-safe preview,
- delete and withdraw,
- report and block,
- moderation review,
- user-generated content status,
- AI-assistance status,
- commercial disclosure,
- abuse and impersonation controls,
- third-party privacy protection.

### 4.10 Experience Provenance and Evidence Layer

Purpose:

- distinguish lived experience from editorial, synthetic demo, AI-assisted, partner, public-resource, or commercial content,
- prevent popularity from being mistaken for truth,
- preserve source, freshness, limitations, and moderation status.

Every surfaced experience should carry an internal provenance record. Demo or synthetic content must never be presented as a real user's lived experience. Helpfulness feedback is not clinical efficacy or universal proof.

### 4.11 Demand Intelligence Layer

Inputs may include:

- state distributions,
- experience themes,
- saves,
- try intent,
- tried actions,
- helpfulness,
- recommendation behavior,
- report behavior,
- local signals,
- partner interest,
- concept-product feedback.

Outputs may include:

- internal intelligence brief,
- product opportunity brief,
- resource gap summary,
- supplier opportunity brief,
- commercial insight report,
- public-value memo.

External outputs must remain anonymized, aggregated, evidence-labeled, and founder-approved.

## 7. Japan Product Architecture

Yorisou v0.3 uses one Japan-focused product architecture.

### Core shared services

- UserProfile;
- LINE and email AuthIdentity;
- consent and privacy controls;
- Life-State Graph;
- Experience Graph;
- Recommendation Graph;
- trust and visibility controls;
- reports and demand intelligence.

### User-facing surfaces

- Japanese Web product;
- LINE registration, linking, save, and return flows;
- email registration, authentication, and account recovery;
- public-safe share links.

### Operating principle

Do not create international market forks, country-specific communities, overseas data programs, or multilingual product operations during the current stage. Build the Japan product clearly and prove use before considering expansion.

## 8. First-Phase MVP and Eligibility

The first v0.3 MVP should support:

1. Japanese Web entry and clear product explanation.
2. LINE or email registration and login.
3. Existing test and reflection entry points that pass compatibility review.
4. Private state space.
5. Structured experience-card creation.
6. User-selected visibility, including private and anonymous sharing.
7. Direct publication after registration without manual pre-approval.
8. State-based experience discovery.
9. Save, try, helpful, not-for-me, report, block, edit, and delete actions.
10. Basic automated checks and post-publication moderation tools for spam, abuse, privacy exposure, and clearly prohibited content.
11. Recommendation and report bridges.
12. Core analytics and demand-signal capture.

The first release does not require an adult-only gate.

Open registration does not remove the need for terms acceptance, privacy notice, reporting, blocking, deletion, rate limits, abuse controls, and administrative takedown capability.

The first release should not include unrestricted private messaging, follower competition, public popularity rankings, or an infinite feed.

## 9. Business Model Stress Tests

### Stress Test 1

Can Yorisou create value without public posting?

Required answer: yes, through private state, tests, discovery, recommendations, and reports.

### Stress Test 2

Can Yorisou create value without conventional social metrics?

Required answer: yes, through save, try, outcome, relevance, and return.

### Stress Test 3

Can Yorisou monetize without advertising-feed pressure?

Required answer: yes, through reports, premium reflection, recommendation, services, and intelligence.

### Stress Test 4

Can AI assist without fabricating authenticity?

Required answer: only with explicit provenance, user approval, and no invented lived experience.

### Stress Test 5

Can the Japan product remain manageable for one founder?

Required answer: only through one core, staged locales, strict deferral, and automation that reduces workload.

## 24. Unified Multi-Channel Product Architecture

YORISOU must operate as one core platform with channel adapters for Web, LINE/LIFF, iOS, and Android. Email remains an identity, recovery, and service-communication channel.

The shared core includes:

- UserProfile and linkable AuthIdentity;
- consent, privacy, and visibility;
- Life-State, Experience, Recommendation, Personal Memory, and Trust graphs;
- reports, companion services, moderation, analytics, and demand intelligence;
- stable backend APIs and versioned client contracts.

Channel-specific presentation may differ, but facts, user rights, visibility, purchase status, memory, moderation state, and recommendation provenance must remain consistent.

The mobile delivery approach must be selected after repository audit. React Native/Expo, Capacitor, a PWA bridge, shared TypeScript domain code, or another approach may be evaluated. No framework is approved merely by this doctrine.
